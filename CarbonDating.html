The Science Behind It — In Plain Language

What is Carbon-14?

All living things contain carbon. Most of it is ordinary carbon-12 — stable and harmless. But cosmic rays from space constantly bombard Earth's atmosphere, creating a tiny amount of carbon-14 in the air — roughly one C-14 atom for every trillion C-12 atoms. Plants absorb it. Animals eat plants. Everything alive maintains this same ratio. When something dies, intake stops and C-14 slowly decays.

The Half-Life — Nature's Perfect Clock

Carbon-14 decays at a perfectly constant rate: every 5,730 years, exactly half the remaining atoms decay into nitrogen gas. This rate has never changed in the history of the universe. No heat, no pressure, no chemical reaction can speed it up or slow it down. That makes it a perfect, tamper-proof clock.

Remaining C-14 = (½) ^ (Age ÷ 5,730)
5,730 years → 50% · 11,460 years → 25% · 17,190 years → 12.5% · 22,920 years → 6.25%
To find the age: Age = −ln(remaining fraction) ÷ λ   where λ = ln(2) ÷ 5,730

How Scientists Actually Measure It

A few milligrams of sample — a splinter of wood, a fragment of bone, a scrape of charcoal — go into an Accelerator Mass Spectrometer (AMS). This machine separates carbon atoms by weight and counts them individually. The ratio of C-14 to C-12 atoms tells the computer exactly how much C-14 has decayed, and therefore how long the clock has been ticking.

Example — Lascaux Cave Painting, France:
Scientists scraped a tiny amount of charcoal pigment from the cave wall. The AMS found 13.8% C-14 remaining.
Age = −ln(0.138) / (ln(2)/5730) = ~17,000 years old
These paintings were made during the last Ice Age, when woolly mammoths still roamed Europe.

Why Carbon Dating Cannot Date Dinosaurs

This is the most common misconception. Dinosaurs lived 66–230 million years ago. After just 10 half-lives (~57,000 years), C-14 drops below 0.1% — undetectable by any instrument. There is simply nothing left to measure. For ancient rocks and fossils, scientists use other radioactive isotopes with much longer half-lives.

What IS used for dinosaurs:
Uranium-238 → Lead-206 (half-life 4.5 billion years) — used for rocks millions of years old
Potassium-40 → Argon-40 (half-life 1.25 billion years) — used for volcanic rock layers
Scientists date the rock layers above and below the fossil to bracket the age.
